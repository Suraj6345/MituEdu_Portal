Animal Classification

Q1.

What are the criteria for the New System of Classification?

  • Levels of Organization: Cellular, Tissue, Organ, or Organ-system level.
  • Body Symmetry: Asymmetrical, Radial, or Bilateral symmetry.
  • Body Cavity (Coelom): Acoelomate, Pseudocoelomate, or Eucoelomate.
  • Germ Layers: Diploblastic or Triploblastic.
Q2.

Give the characteristics of Phylum Arthropoda.

  • This is the largest phylum in the animal kingdom.
  • These animals have jointed appendages.
  • Body is triploblastic, bilaterally symmetrical, and segmented.
  • Exoskeleton is made of Chitin. (e.g., Cockroach, Butterfly, Crab).
